Paul Revere was a colonial minuteman and a silversmith who lived in Boston during the American Revolution. On April 18, 1775, he received a message from Captain John Parker, the commander of the militia in Concord, Massachusetts, warning him that British troops were on their way to Concord to seize military supplies.
Revere set out immediately on horseback to warn the American militia of the approaching British forces. He rode through the countryside, shouting "The regulars are coming out! The regulars are coming out!" as he went. Revere's message was relayed to other riders who continued to spread the word throughout Massachusetts and beyond.
Revere's ride was one of the most famous events of the American Revolution and is still remembered today. However, it is important to note that Revere did not actually see the British forces himself. He received his information from Parker and other sources and then rode out to warn the militia.
