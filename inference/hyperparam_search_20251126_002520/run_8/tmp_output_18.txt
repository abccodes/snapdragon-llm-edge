Gum contains various ingredients such as sweeteners and flavorings which are not easily digested by the body. When swallowed whole or in small pieces, these components can cause blockages or irritation to occur within the gastrointestinal tract. In some cases, this may lead to serious complications including perforation of the stomach lining or obstruction.
If you accidentally swallow gum while eating, it is generally recommended that you wait for a few hours before attempting to remove it from your body through vomiting. However, if there are any signs of distress such as severe abdominal pain, nausea, or difficulty breathing, seek medical attention immediately.

It's important to avoid eating large amounts of sugary snacks like chewing gum in order to minimize your risk of experiencing adverse effects on your health.
