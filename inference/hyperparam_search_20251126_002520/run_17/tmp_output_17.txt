The newspaper reports that experts say you shouldn't swim straight after eating. Instead, they recommend waiting at least two hours before diving into the pool or sea.
But why is it so important to avoid immediate exercise after a meal?
Experts explain that when we eat food, our bodies need to work hard to digest it and absorb nutrients from what we've eaten. This means that blood flow to the digestive organs increases significantly.
As soon as you start swimming, your body will try to pump more blood to the muscles in your legs for the activity. However, this can lead to a drop in blood pressure, causing dizziness or even fainting.
In addition, swimming immediately after eating may also increase the risk of indigestion and heartburn, especially if you're prone to these conditions.
Therefore, it's recommended that you wait at least two hours before engaging in any physical activity after eating. This will allow your body enough time to digest your food properly and avoid potential discomfort or health issues.
