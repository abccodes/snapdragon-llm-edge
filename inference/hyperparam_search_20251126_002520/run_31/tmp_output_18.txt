Gum contains various ingredients such as plastic and flavorings that are not easily digested by the body. When swallowed, these components can become lodged in the digestive system, causing blockages or irritation to sensitive areas like the esophagus or stomach.
In some cases, swallowing gum may also cause an allergic reaction or respiratory issues if it is aspirated (inhaled into the lungs). These symptoms can be severe and require immediate medical attention.
To prevent these problems from occurring, it's important to follow proper dental hygiene practices and dispose of used chewing gum properly. Chewing gum should not be swallowed under any circumstances, as this could cause serious health issues.
If you or someone else has swallowed a piece of gum and is experiencing symptoms such as chest pain, difficulty breathing, or severe abdominal discomfort, seek medical attention immediately.
