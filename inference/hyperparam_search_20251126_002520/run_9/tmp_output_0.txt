Watermelons are one of our favorite fruits and people often enjoy their flesh but rarely consider what happens to the fruit after consumption. It's also common for children who like watermelon seeds to eat them directly, without considering that they can pose a danger if eaten improperly.

Eating too many melon seeds is likely not safe because melons contain oxalates which are harmful when consumed in large quantities and cause kidney stones or gallstones, as well as irritate the stomach lining. Oxalic acid also blocks calcium absorption from food.

Watermelon seeds have been linked to a number of health problems including digestive issues such as constipation, diarrhea, gas formation, heartburn, irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), ulcers and inflammation in some cases.
If you eat too many melon seeds or are allergic to them it can cause severe abdominal pain. It may also lead to nausea or vomiting.
