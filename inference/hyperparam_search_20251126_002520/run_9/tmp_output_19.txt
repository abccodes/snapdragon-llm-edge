 This is one question that many people ask themselves. There have been countless studies done on how beneficial apples can be for your health, so let's take a closer look at what makes them such great snacks.
Apples contain fiber which helps keep you full and satisfied longer than other foods. Fiber also aids in digestion by moving food through the digestive tract more quickly and efficiently, preventing constipation.
Another benefit of eating an apple is its high content of antioxidants like vitamin C and flavonoids that help protect against damage to your cells caused by free radicals. Free radical damage can lead to a variety of health problems including heart disease and cancer.
Apples also contain phytonutrients which are compounds found in plants with antioxidant properties. These include quercetin, catechins, anthocyanidines, isoflavones, and resveratrol that have been shown to provide various benefits such as improved brain function, reduced inflammation, lower blood pressure, increased energy levels, better sleep quality, enhanced immune system response.
