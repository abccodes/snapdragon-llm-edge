Hemoglobin is blue because it absorbs red light, and reflects blue light. When light passes through the skin, it is absorbed by the hemoglobin in the blood in the veins, and the blue light is reflected back to the eye. This is why veins appear blue to us. However, veins do not appear blue all the time. They can appear red or purple depending on the amount of light and the color of the surrounding objects. For example, in bright sunlight, veins may appear red because the light reflects off the skin and the surrounding objects. In dim light or in the presence of dark objects, veins may appear purple because the light is absorbed more by the hemoglobin and less reflected back to the eye. Overall, the reason why veins appear blue is due to the absorption of red light by hemoglobin and the reflection of blue light back to the eye. [end of text]
