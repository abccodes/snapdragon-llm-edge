 Yes, MSG ( monosodium glutamate, has been scientifically proven to be harmful to humans in small doses. A large number of animal experiments have shown that MSG can cause serious damage to the nervous system. In addition, many human cases of "Chinese restaurant syndrome" have also been reported, which is characterized by headache, flushing, and other symptoms after eating MSG-rich foods. Therefore, it is not advisable to eat too much MSG-rich foods. However, some studies have also found that moderate consumption of MSG may have some health benefits, such as improving memory and cognitive function. Therefore, the key is to find a balance in the intake of MSG. [end of text]
