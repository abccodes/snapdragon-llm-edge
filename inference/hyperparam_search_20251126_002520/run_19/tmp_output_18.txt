What Happens When You Swallow Gum?
When you swallow a piece of gum, your body goes through several stages to break down and eliminate it. The first step is for the saliva in your mouth to begin breaking down the sugar in the gum. This process can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour.
As the gum travels down your esophagus, it will eventually reach your stomach where digestive juices will help further breakdown any remaining sugars or other components of the gum. The undigested parts of the gum may then pass through your intestines and be eliminated in your stool.
While swallowing small amounts of gum is generally not harmful, ingesting large quantities can cause problems such as blockages or obstructions in the digestive tract. This can lead to symptoms like abdominal pain, vomiting, constipation, or even perforation of the intestinal wall in severe cases.
