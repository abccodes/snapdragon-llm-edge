The Declaration of Independence was officially signed on August 2, 1776. However, it was not until July 4, 1776, that the document was adopted by the Continental Congress. The signing of the document took place over a period of several months, with representatives from each state signing the document on different dates. The last state to sign was New York, on October 25, 1776. The Declaration of Independence was one of the most important documents in American history, as it served as a statement of the colonies' intention to separate from Great Britain and establish a new nation.
