Gum contains plastic and other synthetic materials which are not digestible by humans. When swallowed, these substances can block or irritate parts of the gastrointestinal tract such as the esophagus, stomach, intestines, and even the rectal area. This may lead to symptoms like abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, constipation, bloating, cramping, diarrhea, bleeding from the anus (in severe cases), perforation of internal organs, or in extreme situations can cause death.
In addition, gum contains sugar which if swallowed by children and pets can promote tooth decay. It is also important to note that chewing on large pieces of hard candy like lollipops may lead to similar problems as swallowing whole chunks of gum.
If you suspect someone has ingested a piece or entire roll of gum, it's best not to induce vomiting unless instructed otherwise by medical professionals. Instead, seek immediate help from your doctor or emergency services for proper treatment and management of the situation.
