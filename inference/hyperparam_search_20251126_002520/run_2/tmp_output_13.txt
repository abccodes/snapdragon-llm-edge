: If an Earthworm were cut into two halves, each segment will continue growing. The reason for this behavior lies within the structure of their bodies.
Earthworms have a unique body plan that allows them to regenerate lost segments and maintain homeostasis (balance) in various environmental conditions. Their segmented nature is one key factor contributing to their regenerative capabilities.

Each Earthworm has multiple sets of muscles, nerves, digestive organs, circulatory systems, etc., within each segment. When an earth worm gets cut into two halves, the lower half will continue growing because it contains all necessary components for growth and survival.
However, if you were able to remove both heads from a segmented organism like this one, then that would be fatal as they require their central nervous system (brain) in order to survive. The brain is located at the head of an earthworm.

So yes, each segment will continue growing after being cut into two halves.
It's worth noting though that Earth worms have limited regenerative capabilities and can only regenerate certain parts like limbs or segments. They cannot fully replace their entire body if it gets completely severed. [end of text]
