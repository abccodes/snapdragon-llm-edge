What happens if you swallow gum?
When you swallow gum, it typically passes through your digestive system without causing any immediate harm. However, there are a few potential dangers associated with swallowing gum that you should be aware of:
1. Obstruction: If you swallow a large piece of gum, it may become lodged in your esophagus or intestines, causing an obstruction. This can be dangerous and may require medical intervention to resolve.
2. Adhesion: If you swallow a piece of gum, it may stick to the lining of your esophagus or intestines, causing irritation or inflammation. This can be uncomfortable and may require medical treatment to resolve.
3. Toxicity: While gum itself is not toxic, some types of gum may contain harmful chemicals or additives that can be harmful if swallowed in large quantities. For example, some types of gum may contain artificial sweeteners or flavorings that can cause digestive problems if swallowed
