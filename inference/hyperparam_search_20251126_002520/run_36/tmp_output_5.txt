The reason for waving a cape in front of the bulls during bullfights was to help the matador see his target better.
In ancient times, when they first started using horses and swords against wild animals like lions and tigers, people would often wear colorful cloaks as protection from their attacks. The red color helped them stand out so that hunters could track them down more easily.
When bullfighting became popular in Spain around the 17th century, it was natural for matadors to continue using these brightly colored capes during fights with bulls. This made it easier and safer for both of them to see each other clearly on a busy battlefield!
The red cape also has symbolic meaning as well. It represents courage and bravery, which are important qualities that the bullfighter strives to have in order to succeed at his craft.
