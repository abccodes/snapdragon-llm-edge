 Why should we be careful about sitting too closely to a TV?
The distance between an object and its image is inversely proportional to the product of their magnification. For example, if I were standing closer to my computer screen than usual (let's say 18 inches), then the size that appears on it would become smaller.
Therefore, if we sit too close to a television set, our eyes will have to work harder in order for us to see clearly because of its small magnification. This can cause eye strain and discomfort over time. In fact, some studies suggest that sitting closer than 27 inches from the TV screen may increase your risk for developing myopia or nearsightedness.
In addition, if you sit too close to a television set it could also affect sound quality because of its proximity to speakers which can cause distortion and interference with audio. Therefore, we should be careful about sitting too closely to our televisions in order maintain good eye health as well as ensure that the viewing experience is optimal for us.
