The newspaper reports that experts recommend waiting at least two hours after having your dinner before getting into the water. The advice is apparently based on studies which found swimmers who ate just before entering the pool are more likely to get cramps or suffer digestive problems, such as indigestion and heartburn.
However, this advice appears to be contradictory with other reports suggesting you should wait only one hour between eating a meal and swimming.
The Mail's article cites Dr. Edward Gajdusek from Harvard Medical School who says "You can swim immediately after finishing dinner". He adds that the time it takes for food to digest is highly individual, so some people may be able to safely swim right away while others will need more time.
Dr. John Berardi from Precision Nutrition agrees with Dr. Gajdusek and states that he has never seen a problem in his 20 years of coaching people on the subject.
The Daily Mail also cites a study published in the Journal of Physical Therapy which found no evidence to support the theory that waiting two hours before swimming after eating is beneficial for swimmers' performance or health.
