Another reason for color-changing in chameleons is communication. Different colors can convey different messages or emotions, such as aggression, fear, submission, mating signals, and dominance displays. Chameleons use these signals to communicate with other members of their species or potential mates during courtship rituals.
Chameleons also change colors for camouflage purposes. They can blend in with their surroundings by changing the color and pattern on their skin, which helps them avoid predators or hunt prey more effectively.
