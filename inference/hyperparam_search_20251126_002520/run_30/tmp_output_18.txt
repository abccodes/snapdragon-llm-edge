Swallowing Gum: What Happens?
When you swallow gum, it travels down your esophagus and into your stomach. The majority of gum is made up of natural rubber or synthetic polymers that are indigestible by the human body. While most people will not experience any immediate symptoms after swallowing gum, some may experience indigestion or a feeling of bloating due to the presence of the indigestible material.
In rare cases, swallowing large quantities of gum can cause blockages in the esophagus or intestines. These blockages can be serious and require medical intervention to resolve. Symptoms of a blockage include chest pain, difficulty breathing, and vomiting.
What You Can Do If You Swallow Gum
