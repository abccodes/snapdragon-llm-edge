When someone swallows chewing gum, the first thing to happen is that the saliva from within begins breaking down any sugars or other substances present in the chewed-up piece of candy. This process helps dissolve some of the sugar content and makes it easier for digestion.

From there, the chewed-up piece of candy moves through a series of organs including the stomach, small intestine, large intestine, rectum, and finally out of the body.
