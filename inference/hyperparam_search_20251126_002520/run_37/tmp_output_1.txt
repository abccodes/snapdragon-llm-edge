 Fortune Cookies originated in China and were brought to the United States by Chinese immigrants. They have become popular as a dessert at restaurants, bakeries, and special occasions such as weddings and birthdays.

Fortune Cookie Facts:

1. The first known recipe for fortune cookie was published in 1894.
2. Each piece of paper inside is meant to be kept secret until the person has eaten it.
3. Fortune cookies are traditionally served with a small red bean paste called "red pea" or mungbean pastelike filling.

Fortune Cookie History:

The history behind fortune cookie can trace back as far as 1894 when they were first introduced in San Francisco, California by Chinese immigrants who owned bakeries and confectionaries. They became popular among the Asian-American community due to their sweet taste and positive messages.
In Japan, where fortunes are traditionally written on slips of paper called "omikuji," fortune cookies have become a symbol for good luck and positivity.

Fortune Cookie Variations:

There are many variations of Fortune Cookies available today such as chocolate chip filled ones, peanut butter-filled ones, and even fruit-flavored varieties. They can be enjoyed with hot or cold tea and make an excellent dessert option at special occasions
