Fortune Cookies are usually made from flour dough or rice paper wrapped around sweetened almond paste filled with fortunes written on slips of paper inside. They were first introduced into America by Japanese immigrants during World War II as a way for people to express their hopes and wishes in the form of good luck messages.
Today, fortune cookies have become popular all over the world and are often given out at special occasions such as weddings or New Year's Eve celebrations. Many restaurants now offer them on their menus as an extra treat for customers. Fortune Cookies continue to be a beloved tradition around the globe today! [end of text]
