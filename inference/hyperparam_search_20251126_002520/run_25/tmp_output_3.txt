 This question has been asked many times, and there are several answers to this. Some people believe that it's the seeds or even inside the fruit itself while others think it’s just on the outside.
The truth is, all parts of a chile contain capsaicin which makes them spicy. However, some areas have more than others due to how they grow and where they are harvested from.
Capsaicin concentration in chili peppers varies depending on factors such as climate conditions during growth, soil composition, type of plant variety, harvesting methods used, etc., so it's hard to say definitively which part is the spiciest. However, here’s a general guide:
1) Seeds: Capsicins are found mostly inside seeds and in some cases can be concentrated on them.
2) Inside fruit (pulp): The flesh of chile peppers contains capsaicin as well but it's usually less potent than other parts due to the presence of water.
