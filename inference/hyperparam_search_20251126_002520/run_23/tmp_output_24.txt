Nixon's famous "I am not a crook" statement came in response to questions about his involvement with Watergate. The Watergate scandal began as an investigation into the break-in at Democratic National Committee headquarters at the Watergate office complex on June 17, 1972. It quickly expanded to include allegations of Nixon's involvement in cover-ups and obstruction of justice.
The statement was made during a televised address to the nation on July 23, 1974, just days before he resigned from the presidency. At the time, it was widely believed that there were tapes of conversations between Nixon and his aides discussing their plans for covering up Watergate. The implication was that Nixon had something to hide.
Nixon's statement was seen as a defiant refusal to acknowledge any wrongdoing on his part. It also served to reassure the American public that he would not be held accountable for his actions, regardless of what evidence might come to light.
