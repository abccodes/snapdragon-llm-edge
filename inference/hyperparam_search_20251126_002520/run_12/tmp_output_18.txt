What Happens If You Swallow Gum?
When you swallow a piece of chewing gum, your body typically passes through most of it without any issues. The digestive system is designed to break down food into smaller components that can be absorbed by the intestines.
However, some people may experience discomfort or pain after swallowing gum due to its indigestible nature. This can cause bloating and gas in the stomach, which may lead to cramping or diarrhea.
In rare cases, if a person swallows an excessive amount of chewing gum at once, it could potentially block their intestines. If this occurs, medical intervention is typically required to remove the obstruction before serious complications arise.
