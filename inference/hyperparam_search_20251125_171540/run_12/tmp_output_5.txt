By Ana Bernal, 4th grade teacher at El Monte Unified School District and author of "The Colorful World: An Intercultural Reading Journey"
Why do matadors wave red capes? That is a very interesting question! A good way to answer it would be to talk about the history of bullfighting. In the old times, in Spain, people used to wear white clothes because they were afraid of bulls. But as time went by and many people learned more about how to handle the animals, it became safer for them to wear bright colors like red.
Matadors use red capes during a bull fight because it makes the bulls angry and aggressive. The matador's goal is to make sure that all the bulls are gone after the show, so he needs to confuse them using different types of objects. He waves a cape, but also uses other weapons such as swords, sticks and lances.
