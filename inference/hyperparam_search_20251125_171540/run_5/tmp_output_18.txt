What Happens If You Swallow Gum?
When you swallow gum, it typically passes through your digestive system without causing any harm. However, there are a few things that can happen if you swallow too much gum at once:
1. Blockage: If you swallow a large amount of gum at once, it may become lodged in your esophagus or intestines, causing a blockage. This can be dangerous and require medical intervention.
2. Digestive issues: Swallowing gum can cause digestive issues such as indigestion, bloating, and constipation. These symptoms are usually temporary and will resolve on their own.
3. Allergic reaction: Some people may have an allergic reaction to gum, which can cause symptoms such as hives, swelling, or difficulty breathing. If you experience any of these symptoms after swallowing gum, seek medical attention immediately.
