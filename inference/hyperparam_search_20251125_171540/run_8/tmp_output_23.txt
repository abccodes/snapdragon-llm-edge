Paul Revere was a colonial minuteman and a silversmith who lived in Boston during the American Revolution. On April 18, 1775, he received a message from Captain William Dawes to ride through the countryside of Massachusetts and warn the American militia of the approach of British forces.
Revere's famous warning was "The regulars are out!" He then rode on horseback towards Concord, Massachusetts, where he met with other riders including Samuel Adams and John Hancock. Together, they warned the militia of the approaching British troops.
However, Revere's warning was not entirely accurate. The British troops were not as close as he had initially thought, and they did not arrive in Concord until much later. Nevertheless, Revere's warning played a crucial role in alerting the American militia and helping them to prepare for the Battle of Lexington and Concord, which was fought on April 19, 1775.
