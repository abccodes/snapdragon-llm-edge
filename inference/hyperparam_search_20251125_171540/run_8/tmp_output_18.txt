Gum is made of a synthetic rubber called polyvinyl chloride (PVC), which is not digestible by the human body. When you swallow gum, it can get stuck in your stomach or intestines and cause blockages or other complications.
In some cases, swallowing gum can lead to serious medical problems such as perforation of the stomach or intestines, obstruction of the airway, or even death. That's why it's important to be aware of the potential dangers of swallowing gum and to take steps to prevent it from happening.
If you swallow gum, don't panic! Most people are able to pass the gum through their digestive system without any problems. However, if you experience severe abdominal pain, vomiting, or other symptoms, you should seek medical attention immediately.
