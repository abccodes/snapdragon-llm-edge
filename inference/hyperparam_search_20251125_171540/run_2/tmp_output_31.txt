The first moon landing was on July 20, 1969, when NASA's Apollo 11 mission landed the Eagle lunar module on the lunar surface. The mission was piloted by Neil Armstrong, Michael Collins, and Buzz Aldrin. Armstrong and Aldrin descended to the lunar surface in the Eagle, while Collins orbited the moon in the command module.
Armstrong became the first person to set foot on the moon, declaring "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind." Aldrin followed, becoming the second person to walk on the moon. The pair spent about two and a half hours on the lunar surface, collecting scientific samples and conducting experiments.
The Apollo 11 mission was a significant achievement in the history of space exploration. It demonstrated the technological capabilities of NASA and set the stage for future space missions. The moon landing also captured the imagination of people around the world and inspired many to pursue careers in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics.
