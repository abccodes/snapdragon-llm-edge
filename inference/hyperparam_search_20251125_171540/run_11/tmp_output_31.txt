The Apollo program was launched by NASA as part of President John F. Kennedy's challenge to put a man on the Moon before the end of the decade. Over the course of six missions, from 1968 to 1972, American astronauts successfully landed on the lunar surface and conducted various experiments.
One of the most famous Apollo moon landings was that of Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin in July 1969. During their mission, they became the first humans to walk on the Moon's surface. They spent about two hours exploring the lunar terrain before returning to the spacecraft for the journey back to Earth.
Many people still have doubts about whether the Apollo moon landings were real or not, but there is overwhelming evidence that supports the fact that these missions did take place. The photographs and videos taken by astronauts during their time on the Moon are widely regarded as authentic, and they provide a clear picture of what it was like to walk on the lunar surface.
