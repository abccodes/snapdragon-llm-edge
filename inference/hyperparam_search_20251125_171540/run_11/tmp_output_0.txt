Watermelon is known for its high water content and refreshing taste. However, it's less common knowledge that watermelons also contain edible seeds. These seeds are small, black, and have a crunchy texture when eaten raw. Despite their tiny size, they provide several health benefits and nutritional advantages.

### Nutritional Content of Watermelon Seeds

1. **High in Fiber**: One cup (about 34 grams) of watermelon seeds contains approximately 2 grams of fiber. This can help with digestion and promote feelings of fullness.
   
2. **Rich in Antioxidants**: The black color of the seeds indicates that they are rich in antioxidants, particularly anthocyanins. These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties and may contribute to overall health.

3. **Good Source of Minerals**: Watermelon seeds contain various minerals such as magnesium, phosphorus, copper, zinc, and selenium. Magnesium is important for muscle function and energy production, while selenium acts as an antioxidant in the body.
