Assistant When Richard Nixon became president in January 1969, many Americans had high hopes for him. The country was dealing with issues like Vietnam War protests, civil rights unrest, inflation, and economic recession.

Nixon's first State of the Union Address on March 5, 1970, focused on a range of domestic policies. He announced plans to build 36 million additional homes over the next decade, reduce taxes for families with children under six, provide help to those in need, expand job training programs, increase federal funding for education and research, establish new environmental protection rules, and reform social security.

In his statement, Nixon was emphasizing that he was not involved in any illegal activities. He was referring specifically to the Watergate scandal, which had been unfolding since June 1972 when five men were caught breaking into Democratic National Committee headquarters at the Washington Hilton Hotel. The break-in was part of a political campaign fund-raising scheme.
