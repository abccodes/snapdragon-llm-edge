 This is a question that many people have asked at some point in their lives, and for good reason. Swallowing gum can be concerning because it's not something the body naturally processes.
To answer this question properly, we need to understand how gum works and what happens when it gets stuck in our digestive system.
Gum consists of a base material called gumbase, which is typically made from elastomers like polyisoprene or synthetic rubber. This allows the gum to be flexible and chewy. The flavoring agents, such as sweeteners or fruit essences, are added for taste. Additionally, there may be colorants or other additives.
When you swallow a piece of gum, it travels down your esophagus into your stomach. The acids in your stomach help break down the food you eat, but they do not digest the gumbase. Instead, the digestive system passes the unprocessed gum through to your intestines.
In some cases, the gum may pass through your body without any issues and be expelled with your feces. However, this can take several days or even weeks, depending on how much gum you have swallowed.
