 Paul Revere famously rode through Massachusetts on April 18, 1775, warning colonists about an approaching British Army. He is most famous for shouting "The Regulars are out!" as he passed by some houses.
While this quote became part of popular culture and has been referenced countless times in literature, movies, and television shows over the years, it did not actually occur during Revere's original midnight ride.
In reality, what Paul Revere said was much more cryptic. He reportedly shouted "The Regulars are out!" (though there is no definitive record of exactly what he said) as a warning that British troops were approaching Boston from Dorchester Heights.
Revealing the location of his troop movements was considered treason at the time and could have resulted in severe punishment for Revere if caught. As such, he did not want to give away too much information about where the British forces were located or what their intentions were.
