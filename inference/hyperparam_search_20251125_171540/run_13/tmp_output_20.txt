"If we sit too close to the TV, our eyes have to work harder to see everything clearly," she explained. I was surprised to hear that her explanation wasn't quite accurate. My mother always told me that sitting too close to the television would make my eyes 'cross-eyed'. As it turned out, neither of us were entirely right. When children sit too close to a TV or computer screen, they can indeed have eye strain and difficulty seeing things clearly. But crossing their eyes is not one of the results. Instead, when a child sits too close to a television or computer screen, his or her eyes may turn inward slightly, causing what some doctors refer to as 'convergence insufficiency'. This condition makes it difficult for both eyes to work together properly and can cause eye strain or headaches in children. So if you are concerned about your child's TV watching habits, the best thing is probably to remind them to sit back a little.
