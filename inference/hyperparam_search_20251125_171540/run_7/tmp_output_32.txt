Denver International Airport, commonly known as DIA, is located in Jefferson County, Colorado and opened its doors to public traffic on April 26, 1973. It's one of America’s busiest airports, with around 40 million passengers every year. The Denver airport also features an underground shopping mall that runs for about two miles.
However, what many people don’t know is the layer of water beneath it. When construction began on DIA in the late 1960s and early 70’s, workers had to dig through a number of layers before they could reach solid ground. They discovered that one of these layers was water – specifically, groundwater.
The airport's designers then incorporated this layer into their plans for the building. One way they did this was by using it as a foundation for some parts of the airport complex. Additionally, they created an underground drainage system to ensure that any excess water could be safely removed from beneath the runway and other structures.
